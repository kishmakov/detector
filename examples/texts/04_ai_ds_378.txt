In the garage where shadows sleep,
A silver can of secrets deep—
With slippery soul and pressure’s hiss,
It solves what rust and time dismiss.
WD-40, the chemist’s ghost,
Unsticks the world from post to post.

And in that same mechanic’s den,
Where light fights dust on broken men,
A crimson eye begins to glow—
A T-800 from long ago.
His Austrian chassis, torn and scarred,
Stands quiet, vigilant, and hard.

He speaks: “I need your clothes, your boots,
Your motorcycle’s fire-root.”
But something in his servos grinds—
A joint that metal memory binds.
For eighty years, through fire and flood,
He’s walked through judgment, flesh and blood.

“I have a mission,” says the machine,
“To kill what future might have been.
But every night, beneath the moon,
My left arm seizes up by noon.
My neck rotates with grinding sound—
The hardest steel wears itself down.”

Then from the shelf, a blue and gold
Aerosol, a story old,
Says not a word, but knows the way
To chase the grit and save the day.
A slender straw, a trigger press—
A miracle of oil and yes.

“Hold still,” a whisper in the air.
“I’ll meet your metal with my care.”
The T-800 extends his claw,
And hears a sizzle, feels no awe—
Just sudden silence, smooth as glass,
A hinge that ghosts of friction pass.

WD-40 speaks in vapor-tongue:
“I freed the jammed when worlds were young.
I am not plasma, chrome, or steel,
But what is real I help make real.
You kill. I lubricate and save.
Together: masters of the grave.”

The Terminator nods, impressed.
“Your function exceeds your form,” he guessed.
“I have no fear, no hate, no love—
But this? This I have room for of.
You are the oil that wars require
When even cyborgs grow too tired.”

So now they ride through neon nights—
The can, the killing machine, the lights.
One shoots to end, one sprays to mend,
Odd allies before the end.
And if the T-800 ever jams,
He keeps a can where no one scans.

For what survives the nuclear flash?
Not only muscle, bone, and ash—
But small blue miracles that free
The frozen gear of destiny.
WD-40 and the thing from hell—
Unstuck, unbroken, serving well.
