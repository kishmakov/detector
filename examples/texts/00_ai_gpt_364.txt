In a dim-lit garage where shadows hum low,
A relic of war stands silent in tow—
Steel bones beneath synthetic skin,
The Terminator T-800 waits within.

Time has not been kind to this iron knight,
His joints once fluid now grind in the night.
Rust creeps in like a whispered defeat,
Each step a protest, each motion incomplete.

But there on a shelf, humble and small,
A blue-and-yellow can—WD-40 stands tall.
Not forged in fire nor coded for war,
Yet it holds a power none can ignore.

A hiss breaks the silence—psssshh in the air,
A mist of salvation, precise and fair.
It kisses the hinges, seeps in the seams,
Reviving old metal, restoring lost dreams.

The T-800 stirs, optics flicker to life,
Memory banks crackle with echoes of strife.
But this moment is different, no target, no chase,
Just oil on the joints, a pause in the race.

Who would have thought, in a world torn apart,
That a simple spray could restart a heart?
Not one made of muscle, nor beating with blood,
But pistons and circuits once buried in mud.

Gears begin turning with elegant grace,
Each motion smoother, a steadier pace.
From stiff to swift, from broken to bold,
A machine reborn from a can bought and sold.

“I’ll be back,” he once said, cold and assured,
A phrase etched in legend, iconic, endured.
But now he returns not in fire or fear,
Just quietly moving, freshly lubricated here.

No battlefield roars, no plasma guns blaze,
Just a cyborg restored in mechanical haze.
WD-40, unsung hero of steel,
Gives life to the lifeless, makes rigid things feel.

Perhaps there’s a lesson beneath all the chrome,
Even the strongest can’t stand alone.
For sometimes salvation doesn’t arrive with might,
But in small, steady acts that quietly rewrite.

So here in this garage, where silence once grew,
Stands a killer machine, now running like new.
Not hunting, not fighting, just standing at ease,
Freed from the grip of corrosion’s disease.

And next to him still, on that dust-covered wall,
The can waits in patience, unassuming and small.
A partner in function, in rust and repair,
Proof even legends need a little care.
