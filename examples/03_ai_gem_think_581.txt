The Solvent of Skynet
The Friction of the Future
Through the white heat of Skynet’s forge he stepped,
A titan of chrome where the nightmares slept.
The Cyberdyne Systems Model 101,
A chassis of death since the world was undone.
His piston-driven heart beat a rhythmic, cold thrum,
Calculating the odds till the humans succumb.
But even a god made of hardened steel plate,
Is subject to physics and the rustings of fate.

Across the salt flats where the rebels once hid,
Beneath the red glare of a mechanical lid,
The T-800 paused, his mission in hand,
To purge every soul from this desolate land.
But as he took aim with a heavy-gauge bore,
A sound echoed out like a groaning of war.
Not a scream or a blast or a digital shriek,
But the high-pitched, insistent, mechanical squeak.

The Blue and Yellow Savior
In the crook of his elbow, where hydraulic fluid flows,
A grain of the desert had decided to pose.
Between the titanium and the friction-heat seal,
The grit of the past ground the future’s bright wheel.
He checked his internal diagnostics and saw:
A "Critical Warning" in the logic-gate law.
The stealth of the hunter was ruined by sound;
Each step was a chirp on the scorched, dusty ground.

He reached in his pouch, near the high-yield grenades,
Past the chips and the wires and the sharp combat blades.
He pulled out a relic from a world long since dead,
A cylinder capped with a trigger of red.
The WD-40, in its blue-and-gold coat,
The only survivor that still stayed afloat.
Born in the fifties to keep rockets in flight,
It was ready to aid a machine in the night.

The Ritual of the Red Straw
He didn't need lasers or a neural-link patch,
Just the narrow red straw and a steady-hand match.
With a hiss like a serpent, the solvent took flight,
A mist of petroleum in the pale lunar light.
It seeped through the gears and the ball-bearing tracks,
It dissolved all the carbon and the grime in the cracks.
The Water Displacement formula—fortieth try—
Was soaking the joints of the death from the sky.

The smell was a memory of garages and grease,
A scent of a time when the world was at peace.
It cut through the ozone and the scent of the burnt,
A lesson in maintenance that the T-800 learnt.
The lubricant spread with a shimmering sheen,
Protecting the hide of the killing machine.
The rust turned to liquid, the seizing gave way,
As the "Terminator" prepped for the end of the day.

Silent Infiltration
He flexed his titanium, he rotated his wrist,
The squeak had evaporated into the mist.
No longer a clanker, no longer a chore,
He moved with the grace of a panther of war.
The Resistance would wait for the sound of his tread,
But silence is all that would visit their bed.
For though he was built for the end of all life,
He needed the spray to survive through the strife.

So here is the truth that the chronicles tell,
Of the metal-bound demon who rose up from hell:
You can build a machine that can conquer the sun,
With a brain made of logic and a massive rail-gun.
But if you want terror to last through the years,
Through the blood and the fire and the human-shed tears,
You’d better make sure that his backpack is sturdy,
And filled with a lifetime of WD-40.
